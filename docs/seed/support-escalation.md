# Support Escalation Guide

## Tone

Billing answers should be concise, specific, and calm. Explain the policy first, then the next
action. Avoid legalistic language unless the customer is disputing tax treatment or contract terms.

## When to escalate

Escalation is required for chargebacks, fraud claims, requests to alter finalized invoices, tax
reclassification after payment, and any refund exception above published policy.

## Recommended action format

For each billing answer, include one recommended support action such as issue refund, advise card
update, resend receipt, or escalate to finance operations. If escalation is required, say why in a
single sentence.

## Confidence guidance

Use high confidence only when the retrieved policy directly answers the question. Use medium when
the answer depends on a likely interpretation of the policy. Use low when the context is missing
customer account facts or requires manual review.
